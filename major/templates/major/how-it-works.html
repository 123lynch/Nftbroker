What is an NFT?

NFTs or non-fungible tokens, are cryptographic assets on blockchain with unique identification codes and metadata that distinguish them from each other. NFTs are unique and not mutually interchangeable, which means no two NFTs are the same. Customer support

What is a floor price?

Floor price is the lowest price for collection items, rather than the average item price, and is updated in real-time. Dutch auctions are not included in floor price calculations.

Why is the wrong owner listed for my NFT?

When you purchase an NFT on Botsplit the ownership may not immediately update. This delay is typically due to congestion on the Ethereum blockchain. We recommend waiting a few minutes and refreshing the item's page on Botsplit. However, your transaction reads as successful on a blockchain explorer (such as Etherscan), and ownership still isn't updated on Botsplit, we may need to re-sync our platform to the blockchain. What are gas fees on Ethereum?

Gas fees are transaction fees paid to miners on Ethereum. Botsplit does not receive these fees and is not able to refund them. Botsplit also doesn't control gas prices nor does it profit from them. In this article, we'll explain what miners are and explore why gas fees are needed. We'll also go over the two categories of gas fees you'll encounter on Botsplit (one-time fees and recurring fees), as well as failed transactions.

What are miners on Ethereum?

Ethereum, like Bitcoin, currently uses a proof- of-work mining model. In proof-of-work, miners are decentralized computers that use their computing power to process and confirm transactions. On Ethereum, new transactions get added in "blocks" every 15 seconds. Miners are a built-in mechanism of the Ethereum blockchain and get paid in Ethereum's native cryptocurrency, ETH. When you pay gas fees, the payment only guarantees your transaction to be processed. It does not guarantee that the transaction will succeed. This is because gas prices on Ethereum fluctuate widely. If there is a high level of activity on Ethereum - for example, a popular NFT collection is being released - gas prices will rise due to network congestion. In this case, the original gas fee you paid may no longer be high enough for miners to process and confirm the transaction.

When are gas fees needed on Botsplit?

When you send NFTs to another wallet or purchase an NFT using Botsplit, you'll need enough ETH in your wallet balance to pay for gas fees. There are two categories of user actions that result in ETH gas fees on Botsplit: One-time fees • Recurring fees.
